Soft Cream-Cheese Butter Cookies

A quick, one-bowl chocolate cookie with crisp edges, a chewy center, and melty chips in every bite.

Preparation time

  • Total: Approximately 30 minutes

  • Preparation: 12 minutes

  • Cooking: 12 minutes

Ingredients

  • 212 g unsalted butter

  • 44 g cream cheese, at room temperature

  • 245(lightly spooned into the cup and leveled off) bleached all-purpose flour

  • 8 g baking soda

  • 132 g packed light brown sugar

Instructions

  1. Prep the oven & tray: Heat oven to 180 °C / 350 °F. Line a baking tray with parchment.

  2. Cream:In a bowl, beat the unsalted butter and cream cheese until very smooth and fluffy (2–3 min). Add the packed light brown sugar and beat until pale and creamy (1–2 min).

  3. Dry mixed:In a separate bowl, whisk together the bleached all-purpose flour and baking soda to evenly distribute the leavener.

  4. Make the dough:Fold the dry mix into the creamed mixture in 2–3 additions, mixing just until no dry pockets remain. If the dough feels very soft, chill 15–20 minutes to firm slightly.

  5. Portion:Scoop heaped tablespoons (or a small cookie scoop) onto the trays, spacing about 5 cm / 2 in apart. Lightly flatten the tops for even baking.

  6. Bake:10–12 minutes, until the edges are lightly golden.

  7. Cool & serve:Let cookies cool on the tray 5 minutes, then transfer to a rack to cool completely. Enjoy!
